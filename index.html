Introduction

What is AEON?

AEON is a minimal, lore‑forward protocol concept where agents act autonomously, encryption preserves ephemeral secrecy, optimization selects the best routes for cost and latency, and the network coordinates receipts and reputation. It's a story about where intelligent systems might flow next.

  • Agentic: self‑directed micro‑operations across nodes.
  • Encryption: post‑quantum‑minded, ephemeral secrecy primitives.
  • Optimization: real‑time route selection for tasks, latency, and cost.
  • Network: off‑chain execution, on‑chain settlement optional.

Architecture

Protocol Primitives

AR

Agentic Runtime

Policy‑driven micro‑ops with bounded autonomy.

The Agentic Runtime provides a sandboxed execution environment where agents operate under strict policy constraints, enabling autonomous decision-making within predefined boundaries and risk parameters.

EL

Encryption Layer

Ephemeral secrecy; attestations without exposure.

The Encryption Layer implements zero-knowledge attestation mechanisms and ephemeral encryption schemes, ensuring data secrecy throughout the computation lifecycle without ever exposing raw inputs.

OG

Optimization Graph

Multi‑vector route selection across cost/latency/risk.

The Optimization Graph maintains a dynamic topology of compute routes, continuously evaluating trade-offs between cost, latency, bandwidth, and reputation to select optimal execution paths in real-time.

NF

Network Fabric

Transport primitives, quorums, and receipt channels.

The Network Fabric coordinates message passing, consensus formation, and receipt generation across distributed nodes, providing the foundational infrastructure for reliable off-chain execution.
